\n My name is Dr. Ing. Lawrence Farrugia Caruana and I am conducting this research\n together with Dr. Jacqueline Żammit. We are presently carrying out a study\n investigating in-service Maltese language teachers’ perceptions of virtual reality (VR)\n technology as a tool for language teaching. Specifically, the study examines teachers’\n beliefs regarding the value of VR for student learning, their expectations of success in using it,\n and the perceived costs and practical challenges associated with its implementation in the Maltese\n language classroom. The study also explores whether prior experience with VR technology is related\n to these beliefs, and how teachers perceive the potential of VR for teaching Maltese in terms of\n cultural relevance, motivational value, and curricular fit.\n

\n

\n Should you agree to participate, you will be invited to take part in a three-stage process. First,\n you will be asked to complete a brief background questionnaire capturing information about your\n teaching experience, technology use, and any prior exposure to VR. You will then be invited to\n experience a 360-degree interactive Maltese language VR tool individually, for approximately\n 15–20 minutes. Immediately following this experience, you will be asked to complete a\n post-session questionnaire to capture your reflections and perceptions while impressions are fresh.\n Some participants may subsequently be invited to take part in a short follow-up interview; however,\n this is entirely optional and separate consent will be sought at that stage.\n

\n

\n Participation in this study is entirely voluntary; you are free to accept or refuse to participate\n without giving a reason. You are also free to withdraw from the study at any time without needing\n to provide any explanation and without any negative repercussions for you.\n
